    Details:

    On June 22, The Hacker Olympics will descend upon New York once again as 100 Hackers compete to be named top Hacker. While some changes will be introduced to the format of Hacker Olympics 2, the spirit is the same. Too many hackathons are about pitch decks, and dead-end hacks. Not at Hacker Olympics. We pit hackers, men and women, against one another in challenges that test their innovation, creativity, coding, N64 skills and hardware chops in a 10 hour day that many called "The best hackathon experience of my life."

    Unlike V.1 this year hackers will not be forced to compete in teams, though teams may have a strategic advantage. Only individuals will be awarded 3rd, 2nd and Top Hacker. Also new this year, hackers can choose to compete within "tracks" or cohorts of other hackers of similar skill-sets. This way a front-end developer with little to no CS background will not be forced to compete in challenges built for back-end pros. Within those tracks hackers will be able to "pick-off" challenges at their own pace, occasionally getting whisked off into a Hacker Olympics "mandatory event". One "mandatory event" from V1 was Stack Overlfow - in which hackers had to stack 300 red dixie cups higher than their competition in 10 minutes.
